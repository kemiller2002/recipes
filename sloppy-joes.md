# Pro-Level Sloppy Joes (Rich, Balanced, Not Sugary)

Serves 4–6  
Prep: 10 minutes  
Cook: 30–35 minutes

---

## Ingredients

### Base
- 1½ lb ground beef (80/20 preferred)
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (only if beef is lean)
- 1 medium onion, very finely diced
- 1 small green bell pepper, very finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced

### Sauce
- ¾ cup ketchup (good quality)
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1½ tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tbsp yellow mustard
- 1½ tbsp dark brown sugar
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp chili powder
- ¼ tsp cayenne (optional)
- ½ tsp kosher salt (adjust to taste)
- Fresh black pepper
- ¼ cup beef stock (or water)

### Optional “Pro” Add-Ins (choose 1–2)
- 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp bourbon (cook off)
- ½ tsp MSG (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. **Brown the beef**
   - Heat a wide skillet over medium-high heat.
   - Add beef and cook undisturbed until well browned.
   - Break up and continue cooking until deeply browned.
   - Drain excess fat, leaving about 1–2 tbsp in the pan.

2. **Build the base**
   - Add onion and bell pepper.
   - Cook 5–7 minutes until softened and lightly sweet.
   - Add garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.

3. **Fry the tomato paste**
   - Stir in tomato paste.
   - Cook 1–2 minutes until it darkens slightly and smells rich.

4. **Add sauce ingredients**
   - Stir in ketchup, Worcestershire, mustard, brown sugar, spices, beef stock, and any optional add-ins.
   - Reduce heat to medium-low.

5. **Simmer**
   - Simmer uncovered for 15–25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
   - Sauce should be thick, glossy, and spoon-clinging.

6. **Final seasoning**
   - Taste and adjust:
     - More acid: splash of vinegar
     - Too sweet: more mustard or Worcestershire
     - Flat: additional salt or soy sauce

---

## Serving

- Serve hot on **buttered, toasted buns** (brioche or potato rolls recommended).
- Optional toppings:
  - Sharp cheddar or American cheese
  - Pickles or pickled jalapeños
  - Creamy slaw
  - Fried egg (for chaos)

---

## Notes
- Proper browning and cooking the tomato paste are key to depth of flavor.
- This recipe balances sweet, savory, acid, and smoke without being sugary.
- Holds well for leftovers and reheats beautifully.
