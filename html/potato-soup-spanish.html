Potaje de Garbanzos con Chorizo

Spanish Chickpea, Vegetable & Chorizo Stew

A hearty Spanish stew that eats like a complete meal. Smoky chorizo, tender vegetables, chickpeas, and a rich paprika-infused broth come together in a rustic dish perfect for cooler weather.

Yield: 4–6 servings Prep Time: 20 minutes Cook Time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

Base

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 8 oz Spanish chorizo, sliced into rounds
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

Vegetables

  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed

Broth

  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 5 cups chicken stock
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika (pimentón)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Finish

  • 2 cups baby spinach or chopped kale
  • 1–2 tsp sherry vinegar
  • Extra virgin olive oil for serving

Instructions

1. Render the Chorizo

Heat the olive oil in a Dutch oven or heavy soup pot over medium heat.

Add the chorizo and cook for 5–7 minutes until lightly browned and the paprika-rich oils have rendered into the pot.

Remove about one-third of the chorizo and reserve for garnish if desired.

2. Build the Sofrito

Add the onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened.

Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

3. Develop the Flavor Base

Add the smoked paprika and stir for 15 seconds.

Immediately add the crushed tomatoes and cook for 5–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture darkens slightly and becomes concentrated.

4. Add Vegetables and Stock

Add:

  • Carrots
  • Bell pepper
  • Potato
  • Chickpeas

Pour in the stock and add the bay leaf.

Bring to a gentle boil.

5. Simmer

Reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered for 25–30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the broth has slightly thickened.

Season with salt and pepper to taste.

6. Finish

Stir in the spinach or kale and cook for 2–3 minutes until wilted.

Remove from heat and stir in the sherry vinegar.

Discard the bay leaf.

To Serve

Ladle into warm bowls.

Top with:

  • Reserved chorizo
  • A drizzle of good olive oil
  • Crusty bread on the side

Chef's Notes

For More Depth

Add one of the following during the simmer:

  • Ham hock
  • Jamón bone
  • Parmesan rind (not traditional, but excellent)

For a Thicker Stew

Mash ½ cup of the chickpeas and stir them back into the pot.

For More Vegetables

Add:

  • Zucchini
  • Green beans
  • Cabbage
  • Swiss chard

Make Ahead

Like many Spanish stews, this is often better the next day after the flavors have had time to meld.

Key Technique

The most important step is cooking the chorizo first and building the soup on the rendered paprika oil. That fat becomes the foundation of the entire dish and gives the stew its characteristic Spanish flavor.
