Savory Grilled Chicken Wings

Dry-rubbed grilled chicken wings with an optional garlic butter finish.

Main Recipe

Ingredients

Wings

  • 2–3 lbs chicken wings, separated into flats and drumettes
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or canola)

Dry Rub

  • 2 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp sweet paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp coarse black pepper
  • 1 tsp dry mustard
  • 1 tsp celery seed
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp ground sage
  • ½ tsp white pepper
  • 1 tsp light brown sugar (optional)
  • ½ tsp MSG (optional)

Steps

  1. Pat the wings completely dry with paper towels.
  2. Toss the wings with the oil until lightly coated.
  3. Mix all dry rub ingredients together.
  4. Season the wings generously with the rub.
  5. Refrigerate uncovered for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.

Garlic Butter Finish (Optional)

Ingredients

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 garlic clove, finely minced
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 tsp fresh parsley, finely chopped

Steps

  1. Melt the butter over low heat.
  2. Stir in the garlic and cook for about 30 seconds.
  3. Add the Worcestershire sauce and honey.
  4. Remove from the heat and stir in the parsley.

Grilling

Steps

  1. Prepare a two-zone fire with one side at medium-high heat (375–425°F) and the other side for indirect cooking.
  2. Place the wings on the indirect side of the grill.
  3. Close the lid and cook for 20–25 minutes, turning every 5–7 minutes.
  4. Move the wings over direct heat and grill 2–3 minutes per side until the skin is crisp and lightly charred.
  5. Cook until the internal temperature reaches 185–195°F.
  6. If desired, toss the hot wings lightly in the garlic butter before serving.

Notes

  • Refrigerating the wings uncovered overnight helps dry the skin, resulting in a crispier finish.
  • A very light dusting of additional rub during the last 10–15 minutes of grilling adds a fresh burst of flavor.
  • Fruit woods such as apple or cherry complement this seasoning well if cooking over charcoal or in a smoker.
  • The small amount of brown sugar enhances browning without making the wings sweet or burning easily.
  • Wings are at their most tender between 185°F and 195°F, when the connective tissue has fully rendered.
