Pro Grilled Lamb Chops

Restaurant-style grilled lamb chops with a flavorful herb marinade, a beautifully charred crust, and a bright finish.

Servings

4

Best Cuts

  • Rib chops ("lollipops") – most tender and elegant
  • Loin chops – meatier and more steak-like

Aim for chops that are:

  • 1–1½ inches thick
  • Well marbled
  • Frenched for presentation (optional)

Ingredients

Lamb

  • 8 lamb rib chops
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly cracked black pepper

Marinade

  • 5 garlic cloves, grated
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil

For Finishing

  • Flaky sea salt
  • Lemon wedges

Optional:

  • Mint chimichurri
  • Rosemary butter
  • Whipped feta

Instructions

1. Marinate the Lamb

In a bowl, combine:

  • Garlic
  • Rosemary
  • Thyme
  • Lemon zest
  • Dijon mustard
  • Cumin
  • Smoked paprika
  • Red wine vinegar
  • Olive oil

Coat the lamb chops thoroughly.

Cover and refrigerate:

  • Minimum: 1 hour
  • Ideal: 4–6 hours

Remove from the refrigerator 30–45 minutes before grilling.

2. Prepare the Grill

Set up a two-zone fire:

Gas Grill

  • One side on high heat
  • One side on medium-low heat

Charcoal Grill

  • Bank coals to one side

Target temperature:

  • Hot zone: 500–600°F (260–315°C)

3. Grill the Lamb

Pat excess marinade from the chops.

Season lightly with:

  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper

Oil the grill grates lightly.

Sear

Place chops over direct heat:

  • 2–3 minutes on the first side
  • 2 minutes on the second side

Move to the cooler side if additional cooking is needed.

Internal Temperature Guide

| Doneness | Temperature | |-----------|------------| | Rare | 125°F | | Medium-Rare | 130–135°F | | Medium | 140°F |

Remove from the grill about 5°F before your target temperature.

4. Rest and Finish

Rest lamb chops for 5–8 minutes.

Finish with:

  • Flaky sea salt
  • Fresh lemon juice
  • Small drizzle of olive oil or rosemary butter

Serve immediately.

Mint Chimichurri

Excellent with grilled lamb.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • ½ cup fresh mint
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 small shallot
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Method

Finely chop by hand or pulse briefly in a food processor.

Stir in olive oil and season with salt.

Allow to sit for at least 15 minutes before serving.

Chef Tips

Pat Dry Before Grilling

Removing excess marinade improves browning and creates a better crust.

Add a Touch of Smoke

Lamb pairs exceptionally well with:

  • Oak
  • Cherry
  • Rosemary stems

Manage Flare-Ups

Small flare-ups add flavor.

Large flare-ups create bitterness and should be avoided.

Finish with Acid

A squeeze of fresh lemon at the end brightens the rich flavor of lamb.
