Pro Mexican Rice (Arroz Rojo)

A restaurant-style Mexican rice with deep tomato flavor, fluffy grains, and a rich savory finish.

Yield

  • 4–6 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice, rinsed and dried well
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil (or lard)
  • 2 Roma tomatoes
  • ¼ onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 cups warm chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ cup peas and diced carrots (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Equipment

  • Blender
  • Medium saucepan with lid
  • Fork for fluffing

Instructions

1. Prepare the Tomato Base

Combine in a blender:

  • Roma tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Garlic

Blend until smooth.

You should have approximately 1 cup of liquid. If needed, add a little chicken stock to reach 1 cup.

2. Toast the Rice

Heat the oil in a saucepan over medium heat.

Add the dried rice and cook, stirring frequently, until:

  • Golden brown
  • Nutty smelling

This should take approximately 6–8 minutes.

Do not rush this step. Properly toasted rice creates the characteristic flavor and texture of great Mexican rice.

3. Build the Flavor Base

Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1 minute.

Carefully pour in the blended tomato mixture and stir to combine.

Cook for 2–3 minutes until slightly thickened.

4. Add Stock and Seasonings

Add:

  • Chicken stock
  • Salt
  • Cumin
  • Black pepper

Taste the liquid.

The cooking liquid should taste slightly saltier than you want the finished rice.

5. Cook the Rice

Bring to a gentle simmer.

Add peas and carrots if using.

Cover and reduce heat to low.

Cook for 15 minutes without lifting the lid.

6. Rest

Turn off the heat.

Leave covered for 10 minutes.

This allows the rice to finish steaming and stay fluffy.

7. Finish

Fluff gently with a fork.

Stir in chopped cilantro if desired.

Serve immediately.

Chef Notes

For Extra Flavor

Replace the oil with:

  • Chicken fat
  • Bacon fat
  • Pork lard

Restaurant Trick

Add:

  • 1 teaspoon Better Than Bouillon Chicken Base

to the stock before cooking.

Mild Heat

Add a whole serrano pepper to the pot while simmering.

Remove before serving.

The pepper adds flavor without making the rice overly spicy.

Common Mistakes

  • Not drying the rice before toasting
  • Under-toasting the rice
  • Stirring during cooking
  • Opening the lid while simmering
  • Skipping the resting period

Suggested Pairings

  • Carne asada
  • Chicken fajitas
  • Carnitas
  • Enchiladas
  • Hard-shell beef tacos
  • Refried beans
  • Guacamole
  • Pico de gallo
