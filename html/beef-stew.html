Pro Beef Stew

Why It Works

A really good "pro" stew is less about fancy ingredients and more about layering flavor correctly.

The biggest upgrades are:

  • Browning the meat deeply
  • Building a real fond
  • Using gelatin/collagen-rich ingredients
  • Adding umami in small amounts
  • Cooking low and slow
  • Finishing with acid and fresh herbs

Ingredients

Meat

  • 3 lbs beef chuck roast, cut into large chunks
  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper

Aromatics

  • 2 large onions, diced
  • 3 carrots, thick cut
  • 3 celery stalks
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced

Flavor Builders

  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp soy sauce or fish sauce
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard

Liquid

  • 1 cup red wine
  • 4 cups beef stock
  • 1 packet unflavored gelatin (chef trick)
  • Or use homemade stock

Herbs

  • 2 bay leaves
  • Several sprigs fresh thyme
  • Optional: 1 small sprig rosemary

Vegetables

  • 1½ lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, cut into chunks
  • 8 oz mushrooms, halved
  • Optional: pearl onions

Finish

  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1–2 tsp red wine vinegar or sherry vinegar

Method

1. Prepare the Meat

Pat the chuck roast completely dry.

Season generously with:

  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper

Let sit for 20–30 minutes if time allows.

Moisture is the enemy of browning.

2. Brown the Meat

Heat a Dutch oven over medium-high to high heat.

Add a small amount of neutral oil.

Brown the beef in batches:

  • Do not overcrowd the pot.
  • Let a crust develop before turning.
  • Aim for deep brown color, not gray.

Remove browned beef and set aside.

3. Build the Fond

Add:

  • Onions
  • Carrots
  • Celery

Cook until softened and lightly browned.

Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds.

Stir in tomato paste and cook until it darkens to a brick-red color.

4. Deglaze

Add red wine.

Scrape all browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

Reduce wine by approximately half.

Add:

  • Beef stock
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Soy sauce or fish sauce
  • Dijon mustard
  • Gelatin

Stir until combined.

5. Slow Cook

Return beef and any accumulated juices to the pot.

Add:

  • Bay leaves
  • Thyme
  • Optional rosemary

Cook:

  • 325°F (163°C)
  • Covered, slightly ajar
  • 2½–3½ hours

The oven provides more even heat than the stovetop.

6. Add Vegetables

During the final 45–60 minutes of cooking, add:

  • Potatoes
  • Mushrooms
  • Pearl onions (if using)

This prevents them from becoming overcooked.

7. Finish Like a Chef

Just before serving, stir in:

  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1–2 tsp vinegar
  • Fresh parsley

The acidity brightens the stew and balances the richness.

Optional Restaurant-Level Upgrades

Bacon or Pancetta

Before browning the beef:

  • Render 4 oz bacon or pancetta.

Use the rendered fat to cook the vegetables.

Porcini Mushroom Powder

Add:

  • 1 tsp porcini mushroom powder

This adds significant depth and umami.

Charred Onion Trick

Char half an onion in a skillet until deeply blackened.

Add it during the simmer.

Remove before serving.

This creates remarkable depth of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Bread

  • Toasted sourdough
  • Butter
  • Garlic rubbed on warm toast

Alternatives

  • Buttery mashed potatoes
  • Parmesan polenta

Notes

For best flavor:

  • Make the stew a day ahead.
  • Refrigerate overnight.
  • Reheat gently before serving.

Many stews taste even better on the second day.
