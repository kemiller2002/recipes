# Pro-Style Bread Pudding
*Rich, custardy, restaurant-level dessert*

## Why This Works
- Brioche or challah provides fat and structure
- Proper custard ratio prevents sogginess
- Browned butter and vanilla bean add depth
- Optional bourbon sauce elevates it to steakhouse quality

---

## Ingredients (8–10 servings)

### Bread Base
- 1 lb brioche or challah, cubed 1½–2 inches
- 4 tbsp unsalted butter, melted (plus more for dish)

### Custard
- 2 cups heavy cream
- 1 cup whole milk
- 4 large eggs
- 3 large egg yolks
- ¾ cup dark brown sugar
- ¼ cup granulated sugar
- 1 tbsp vanilla bean paste (or seeds of 1 vanilla bean)
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- ¼ tsp nutmeg
- ¾ tsp kosher salt

### Optional Mix-Ins (choose 1–2)
- Toasted pecans or walnuts
- Golden raisins or currants
- Dark chocolate chunks
- Dried cherries

---

## Method

### 1. Dry the Bread
Spread bread cubes on a sheet pan.  
Bake at 300°F for 20–25 minutes until dry but not toasted.

### 2. Brown the Butter
Melt butter over medium heat until nutty and amber.  
Cool slightly.

### 3. Make the Custard
Whisk eggs, yolks, and sugars until smooth.  
Add cream, milk, vanilla, spices, salt, and browned butter.  
Do not whip air into the mixture.

### 4. Assemble
- Butter a 9×13 baking dish
- Add bread and mix-ins
- Pour custard evenly
- Press gently so all bread is soaked
- Rest 30–45 minutes

### 5. Bake
Bake at 350°F:
- 30 minutes covered loosely with foil
- Remove foil and bake 20–25 minutes more

The top should be golden and the center just set.  
Rest 15 minutes before serving.

---

## Optional Bourbon Vanilla Sauce

### Ingredients
- ½ cup heavy cream
- ¼ cup brown sugar
- 2 tbsp butter
- 2 tbsp bourbon
- 1 tsp vanilla
- Pinch kosher salt

### Method
Simmer everything except vanilla for 5–7 minutes until thickened.  
Finish with vanilla.

---

## Serving Notes
Serve warm with bourbon sauce, vanilla ice cream, or whipped mascarpone.  
Optional powdered sugar or brûléed sugar crust for presentation.
