# Moroccan-Inspired Short Rib Compendium

This compendium collects several Moroccan-inspired directions for
braised beef short ribs. Each concept shares the same foundation: deeply
browned short ribs, slow braising, and a sauce reduced until glossy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Classic Moroccan Braised Short Ribs

## Flavor Profile

-   Cumin
-   Coriander
-   Cinnamon
-   Ginger
-   Turmeric
-   Garlic
-   Onion
-   Tomato
-   Beef stock
-   Preserved lemon
-   Green olives
-   Fresh parsley and cilantro
-   Toasted almonds

## Serve With

-   Saffron couscous
-   Preserved lemon mashed potatoes
-   Parsnip purée

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Date & Pomegranate Short Ribs

## Flavor Profile

-   Dates
-   Pomegranate molasses
-   Cinnamon
-   Cardamom
-   Cumin
-   Beef stock

## Finish

-   Fresh pomegranate arils
-   Toasted pistachios
-   Mint

## Character

Rich, savory, and lightly sweet with bright acidity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Ras el Hanout Short Ribs

## Homemade Blend

-   Coriander
-   Cumin
-   Cinnamon
-   Ginger
-   White pepper
-   Black pepper
-   Turmeric
-   Nutmeg
-   Allspice
-   Cardamom
-   Pinch cloves
-   Optional smoked paprika

## Serve With

-   Pearl couscous
-   Roasted carrots
-   Charred onions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Harissa-Glazed Short Ribs

## Method

Braise traditionally.

Reduce the braising liquid.

Finish with:

-   Harissa
-   Honey
-   Butter

Brush onto the ribs and caramelize under a broiler before serving.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Sauce Finish

Reduce the braising liquid until it coats a spoon.

Finish with:

-   Cold butter
-   Fresh herbs
-   Preserved lemon
-   Splash of sherry vinegar or lemon juice

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Signature Concept

A restaurant-style presentation featuring:

-   Ras el Hanout braised short ribs
-   Glossy preserved lemon pan sauce
-   Saffron parsnip purée
-   Roasted rainbow carrots
-   Crispy chickpeas
-   Toasted Marcona almonds
-   Cilantro, parsley, and mint
-   Fresh pomegranate seeds

The result is unmistakably short ribs first, with Moroccan flavors
unfolding in layers rather than overwhelming the beef.
