# 🇨🇺 Pro Cuban Black Beans (Frijoles Negros)

A traditional Cuban-style black bean recipe featuring a proper sofrito, balanced acidity, and a rich, silky texture.

## Yield
6–8 servings

## Ingredients

### Beans
- 1 lb dried black beans
- Water for soaking and cooking
- 1 bay leaf

### Sofrito
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 1 green bell pepper, finely diced
- 4–6 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp ground cumin

### Flavor Build
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp sugar
- 2–3 tbsp red wine vinegar
- Kosher salt, to taste
- Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

### Optional Additions
- 1–2 slices bacon, diced
- 4 oz ham, diced
- Splash of dry white wine
- Fresh cilantro for garnish

## Instructions

### 1. Cook the Beans

1. Rinse and sort the black beans.
2. Soak overnight in plenty of water.
3. Drain and place in a large pot with fresh water and the bay leaf.
4. Simmer until tender, about 1–1½ hours.
5. Reserve the cooking liquid and set the beans aside.

> If using canned beans, use three 15-ounce cans and reserve the canning liquid.

### 2. Build the Sofrito

1. Heat the olive oil in a Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium heat.
2. If using bacon or ham, cook until lightly browned.
3. Add the onion and green pepper.
4. Cook for 6–8 minutes until softened.
5. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute.
6. Stir in the oregano and cumin and cook for 30 seconds.

### 3. Add Depth

1. Stir in the tomato paste.
2. Cook for 2–3 minutes until it darkens slightly.
3. Deglaze with a splash of dry white wine if using.

### 4. Simmer

1. Add the cooked beans.
2. Add enough reserved bean liquid to cover.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer.
4. Cook for 20–30 minutes.
5. Occasionally mash a small portion of the beans against the side of the pot to naturally thicken the sauce.

### 5. Balance the Flavors

1. Stir in the sugar.
2. Add the red wine vinegar.
3. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

The finished beans should be:
- Rich and savory
- Slightly tangy
- Gently sweet
- Deeply earthy

### 6. Adjust Texture

- If too thin, simmer uncovered until reduced.
- If too thick, add additional bean liquid or water.

## Serving Suggestions

Serve with:

- White rice
- Cuban roast pork
- Grilled chicken
- Fried sweet plantains (maduros)
- Pickled onions
- Lime wedges

## Chef's Notes

### Keys to Success

- Save the bean cooking liquid.
- Fully cook the sofrito before adding the beans.
- Don't skip the vinegar.
- Mash a few beans for a naturally creamy texture.
- Season generously with salt at the end.

### Make Ahead

The flavor improves significantly after a day in the refrigerator.

### Storage

- Refrigerator: 4–5 days
- Freezer: Up to 3 months
