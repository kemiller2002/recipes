# Restaurant-Style Lobster Corn Chowder

A rich, creamy chowder that lets the sweetness of the lobster and fresh corn shine without becoming overly thick. Built in layers for a restaurant-quality result.

## Ingredients

### Lobster

- 2–3 lobster tails (or about 1½ lb cooked lobster meat)
- 2 tbsp butter

### Chowder

- 4 slices thick-cut bacon, diced
- 3 tbsp butter
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2 celery stalks, diced
- 1 medium carrot, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 4 cups seafood stock (or chicken stock)
- 2 cups whole milk
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1½ lb Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and diced into ½-inch cubes
- 4 ears fresh sweet corn, kernels removed (or 4 cups frozen corn)
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- Pinch of cayenne pepper (optional)
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

### Finish

- Reserved lobster meat
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2 tbsp chopped fresh chives
- 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
- Reserved crispy bacon

## Directions

1. If using raw lobster tails, gently simmer them for 6–8 minutes until just cooked. Remove the meat from the shells, cut into bite-sized pieces, and reserve. Save the shells if making homemade lobster stock.
2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, cook the bacon until crisp. Remove the bacon and reserve for garnish, leaving about 2 tablespoons of bacon fat in the pot.
3. Add the butter to the bacon fat. Add the onion, celery, and carrot and cook for 8–10 minutes until softened. Stir in the garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and cook for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
5. Slowly whisk in the seafood stock until smooth. Add the bay leaf, thyme, smoked paprika, and cayenne (if using). Bring to a gentle simmer.
6. Add the diced potatoes and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until nearly tender.
7. Stir in the milk and heavy cream. Do not allow the chowder to boil after adding the dairy.
8. Add the corn and cook for another 5–8 minutes until tender but still slightly crisp.
9. Stir in the lobster meat, lemon juice, and most of the bacon. Heat gently for 2–3 minutes, just until the lobster is warmed through.
10. Remove the bay leaf. Taste and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

## Serving Suggestions

- Garnish with the remaining bacon, fresh chives, and parsley.
- Serve with oyster crackers, crusty sourdough, or a buttered grilled baguette.

## Chef Tips

- Fresh summer corn produces the sweetest, most flavorful chowder.
- Yukon Gold potatoes provide a naturally creamy texture while holding their shape.
- Blend about 1 cup of the finished chowder and stir it back into the pot for a thicker, silkier consistency without making the entire soup smooth.
- If you have lobster shells available, simmer them in the seafood stock for 20–30 minutes before making the chowder for significantly deeper lobster flavor.
- A splash of dry sherry added after the vegetables have softened adds excellent depth.

## Restaurant Upgrade

For an even more refined version:

- Brown 2 tablespoons of butter and stir it in just before serving.
- Add 1/2 teaspoon lobster base (such as Better Than Bouillon Lobster Base) if your seafood stock needs more lobster flavor.
- Finish with a few drops of sherry vinegar instead of additional lemon juice for a brighter, more balanced finish.
