# Canonical Professional Biscuits and Gravy

## Philosophy

Elevate the classic through better ingredients and technique, not novelty.

## Ingredients

### Biscuits

- White Lily flour (preferred)
- Baking powder
- Baking soda
- Kosher salt
- Frozen grated butter
- Cold buttermilk

### Gravy

- 1 lb quality breakfast sausage
- 1/4 cup flour
- 2½ cups whole milk
- 1/2 cup heavy cream

### House Spice Blend

- 1¼ tsp black pepper (divided)
- 1/2 tsp rubbed sage
- 1/4 tsp thyme
- 1/8 tsp marjoram
- 1/8 tsp ground ginger
- 1/8 tsp white pepper
- 1/8 tsp dry mustard
- Pinch nutmeg
- Pinch cayenne

### Finish

- Worcestershire (few drops)
- Optional cultured buttermilk splash
- Fresh cracked pepper
- Chives
- Fried sage leaves

## Directions

1. Bake laminated buttermilk biscuits.
2. Brown sausage deeply until fond develops.
3. Add flour and cook 2–3 minutes.
4. Bloom dry spices in the rendered fat.
5. Gradually whisk in milk and cream.
6. Simmer until silky.
7. Finish with Worcestershire, optional buttermilk, fresh pepper, and garnish.

# Technique Guide

## High-impact techniques

- Brown sausage aggressively.
- Preserve fond.
- Toast flour.
- Bloom spices.
- Layer pepper.
- Freeze biscuits before baking.
- Use milk + cream.
- Finish with acid.

# Research Notes

Major findings:

- Layering pepper creates better aroma.
- Sage defines the traditional profile.
- Small amounts of ginger, mustard, marjoram, and nutmeg increase complexity.
- Umami boosters (porcini, white miso, MSG) improve depth without changing identity.
- Regional variants provide inspiration while preserving core structure.

# Troubleshooting

Thin gravy: simmer longer.
Thick gravy: add warm milk.
Bland: more pepper, acid, or umami.
Greasy: too much fat; balance with roux.
