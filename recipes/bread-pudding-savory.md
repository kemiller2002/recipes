# Pro-Style Savory Bread Pudding
*Crisp top, custardy interior, deeply savory*

## Why This Works
- Enriched bread holds custard without collapsing
- Egg-forward custard allows clean slicing
- Layered umami from fat, aromatics, and aged cheese
- High-heat finish creates a crackly top

---

## Ingredients (8 servings)

### Bread Base
- 1 lb brioche, challah, or pullman loaf, cubed 1½–2 inches
- 4 tbsp unsalted butter, browned
- Butter for dish

### Savory Custard
- 2 cups heavy cream
- 1 cup whole milk
- 5 large eggs
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp freshly cracked black pepper
- ¼ tsp white pepper (optional)
- Small grate fresh nutmeg

### Flavor Core
Choose one primary and one secondary.

**Primary**
- 6 oz thick-cut bacon, diced and rendered  
  or  
- 8 oz mushrooms (cremini or shiitake), hard-seared

**Secondary**
- 1 medium shallot, minced
- 2 cloves garlic, micro-planed

### Cheese (Choose One Star)
- Gruyère
- Comté
- Fontina
- Aged white cheddar

### Herbs
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme
- 1 tbsp chives or parsley

---

## Method

### 1. Dry the Bread
Bake bread cubes at 300°F for 20–25 minutes until dry but pale.

### 2. Build the Flavor Base
- Render bacon or sear mushrooms
- Reserve 2 tbsp fat
- Sweat shallot until translucent
- Add garlic for 30 seconds
- Cool slightly

### 3. Make the Custard
Whisk eggs until smooth.  
Add cream, milk, salt, peppers, nutmeg, and browned butter.  
Avoid foaming.

### 4. Assemble
- Butter a 9×13 baking dish
- Layer bread, protein, cheese, herbs
- Repeat once
- Pour custard evenly
- Press gently to soak
- Rest 30–45 minutes

### 5. Bake
Bake at 350°F:
- 30 minutes covered loosely with foil
- Remove foil and bake 20–30 minutes more

Finish at 425°F for 5 minutes for a blistered top.  
Rest 15 minutes before slicing.

---

## Optional Finishes
- Brush top with melted butter before final bake
- Sprinkle Parm and black pepper after baking
- Finish with chive oil or brown butter drizzle

---

## Variations
- Mushroom, Gruyère, and thyme
- Bacon, cheddar, and chive
- Four-cheese with black pepper
- Herb and lemon zest (vegetarian)

---

## Serving Ideas
Serve with roast chicken, brisket, sloppy joes, or as a brunch centerpiece.  
Excellent make-ahead dish that reheats cleanly.
